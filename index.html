Gun Violence Tracker

In the US in 2015 there wer over 51,948 incidents of gun violence resulting in 13,206 deaths. With 329 mass shootings (as of 12/28/14), the topic of gun violence has been forefront in the media this year. Our team set out to create a website that could encapsulates large amounts of data regarding incidents of gun violence and present it to users in a meaningful, simple and perhaps poignant way.

Despite the prevalence of firearm deaths, data around this topic is surprisingly scarce. Because of this, the focus of our website is to highlight this deficiency and implore Congress to fund research.

Code Fellows 301 Team: Heidi Laursen, Maria Romero, Kenny Suh, and James Mason

Take Action

Public data on incidents of gun violence is difficult to come by. The data presented here is not a precise representation of the state gun violence in the United States or abroad - it is only a fraction of the incidents.

In 1993, the New England Journal of Medicine (NEJM) published an article by Arthur Kellerman, "Gun ownership as a risk factor for homicide in the home," which presented the results of research funded by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC).

The article received considerable media attention, and the National Rifle Association (NRA) responded by campaigning heavily against any federal research on gun deaths.

In 1996, Congress included language in the Omnibus Consolidated Appropriations Bill for Fiscal Year 1997 that "none of the funds made available for injury prevention and control at the CDC may be used to advocate or promote gun control."

Since then almost no national research has been done on gun violence. Again, this December Congress passed the 2016 Omnibus Consolidated Appropriations Bill barring funding for gun research by both the CDC and the National Institutes of Health (NIH).

Please contact your state Senator and let them know you want this ban lifted. Research is needed to combat the rising trend of gun violence in the US.
